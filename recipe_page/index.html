Making Jam at Home

Who doesn't love sweet fruit jam made from the ripest in-season fruit? I have both a plum and apricot tree- so delicious! Many neighbors are willing to share an abundance from their trees during stone fruit season, ask around! You can use storebought fruit for these recipes as well.


Photo by Sigmund on Unsplash

Ingredients

  • 4 cups mashed, chopped, or pureed fruit
  • 3 tsp Pomona's Universal Pectin
  • 4 tsp calcium water prepared according to pectin instructions
  • .25 cup lemon juice
  • 1 cup sugar

Needed for Canning

  • 10 Half-pint canning with flats and rings to match your recipe volume.
  • A water bath canner
  • A jar-lifter for safety

Making the Jam

  1. Prepare the calcium water according to pectin instructions.
  2. Wash, pit, and chop, puree, or mash fruit.
  3. Measure 4 cups of fruit into a thick bottomed saucepan.
  4. Bring pan to a boil for 30 minutes, stirring frequently.
  5. Remove pan from heat and stir in lemon juice/ citric acid and calcium water.
  6. Blend sugar with pectin powder.
  7. Return fruit mixture to a boil and stir in the sugar/pectin mixture.
  8. Continue stirring. Once the jam boils again, remove it from the heat.

Canning the Jam

  1. Fill waterbath canner with water and bring to a boil.
  2. Wash jars, lids, and bands and prepare for canning.
  3. Fill hot canning jars with hot jam, leaving 1/4" headspace.
  4. Wipe rims and seal with lids and bands.
  5. Put filled jars in boiling canner for 10 minutes.
  6. Remove jars and allow them to cool for 12 hours.
  7. Rinse cooled jars, carefully removing bands and checking the seals. Enjoy within one year! Jam keeps 3 weeks once opened.
